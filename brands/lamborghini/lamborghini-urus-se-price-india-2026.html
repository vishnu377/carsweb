Welcome to 2026, where the roar of a V8 engine meets the silent power of electricity. The **Lamborghini Urus SE** has officially landed in India, and it is everything we hoped for. For years, the Urus was the king of luxury SUVs in India. Now, with the 'SE' (Special Edition/Hybrid) tag, it has taken performance to a whole new level. In this massive 2500-word review, we will discuss every detail—from its staggering price to its 60km silent EV range.

1. Lamborghini Urus SE Price in India 2026

Let's talk about the most important thing first: the cost. Buying a Lamborghini is not just buying a car; it’s an investment in a lifestyle. The Urus SE is priced higher than the previous Urus S or Urus Performante because of the complex hybrid technology under the hood.

In India, the **ex-showroom price of the Lamborghini Urus SE is ₹4.57 Crore**. However, once you add the RTO registration, luxury insurance, and some basic customizations, the price easily crosses the ₹5.30 Crore mark.

2. The Engine: 800 CV of Pure Madness

The heart of the Urus SE is a **4.0-liter Twin-Turbo V8 engine**. But wait, there is more. Lamborghini has added a powerful electric motor and a 25.9 kWh battery. When they work together, they produce a massive **800 CV (789 HP)** and **950 Nm of torque**.

Why did they do this? It’s simple. The electric motor provides "instant torque." In traditional petrol cars, you have to wait a micro-second for the turbo to kick in. In the Urus SE, the moment you touch the pedal, the car launches like a rocket. It is smooth, terrifyingly fast, and incredibly addictive.

Transmission and Drive

It comes with an 8-speed automatic gearbox that sends power to all four wheels. Lamborghini has updated the "Torque Vectoring" system, which means the car knows exactly which wheel needs more power during a sharp turn. This makes this heavy SUV feel like a small sports car in the corners.

"Driving the Urus SE is like being in a movie. One moment it's silent like a ghost, and the next, it roars like a lion. There is nothing else like it on Indian roads."

3. Speed and Performance: Faster Than Ever

Numbers don't lie. The Urus SE is faster than the Urus S. Here are the performance stats that will blow your mind:

  • 0 to 100 km/h: Just 3.4 Seconds.
  • 0 to 200 km/h: 11.2 Seconds.
  • Top Speed: 312 km/h.

Even though it weighs more than 2,500 kg because of the heavy battery pack, it is still one of the fastest SUVs in the world. It can beat many dedicated two-seater supercars in a drag race.

4. EV Mode: The Silent Supercar

The biggest change in the 2026 model is the **EV Mode**. You can now drive your Lamborghini to the office or the gym without waking up the neighbors. On a full charge, the Urus SE can travel up to **60 kilometers on pure electricity**.

In EV mode, the top speed is limited to 135 km/h. This is perfect for city driving. Imagine driving through Mumbai or Delhi traffic in total silence, with zero emissions. When the battery runs low, the V8 engine automatically turns on to take over.

Pro Tip: Most Urus owners in India drive less than 30km a day. This means you could potentially drive your Lamborghini for weeks without using a single drop of petrol if you charge it every night!

5. Exterior Design: What’s New?

At first glance, it looks like a standard Urus, but look closer and you will see the 2026 updates. The front hood now has a "floating" design, which means there are no visible lines, making it look very clean. The new LED headlights feature a "C" shape inspired by the Lamborghini Revuelto.

At the back, there is a new spoiler and a giant diffuser that helps keep the car stable at 300 km/h. The 23-inch 'Galanthus' wheels are standard, and they look massive. You can choose from over 100 colors through the Ad Personam program, including some wild neon shades that are very popular in India.

6. Interior Luxury: A Cockpit for Kings

The interior of the Urus SE feels like a high-end private jet. Everything is covered in Alcantara, Carbon Fiber, or Fine Leather. In 2026, the dashboard has been updated with a larger **12.3-inch infotainment screen**.

Key Interior Features:

  • New Graphics: The digital instrument cluster now shows hybrid data, like battery level and energy flow.
  • Comfort: The seats are ventilated, heated, and offer 18-way electric adjustment.
  • Sound: A 21-speaker Bang & Olufsen 3D sound system that sounds like a concert hall.
  • Customization: You can choose the color of the stitching, the seat belts, and even the floor mats.

7. Driving Modes: The Anima Selector

The famous "Anima" lever in the center console lets you change the car's personality. In 2026, there are 11 different combinations of driving modes. The main ones are:

  1. STRADA: For daily driving. Quiet and comfortable.
  2. SPORT: The engine becomes louder and the steering becomes heavy.
  3. CORSA: For the race track. Maximum power and no stability control.
  4. SABBIA (Sand): For driving on beaches or deserts.
  5. EV DRIVE: Pure electric mode for city use.

8. Ground Clearance and Indian Roads

One of the biggest reasons for Urus's success in India is its ability to handle bad roads. The Urus SE comes with **Adaptive Air Suspension**. You can raise the car with a button. At its highest setting, the ground clearance is **248mm**, which is more than many off-road SUVs. This means you don't have to worry about large speed breakers or flooded roads during monsoon.

9. Comparison: Urus SE vs Rivals

The Battle of Titans

In 2026, the Urus SE faces two main rivals: the **Ferrari Purosangue** and the **Aston Martin DBX707**.

  • Ferrari Purosangue: Has a V12 engine but is much more expensive (₹10 Crore+). It's not a hybrid.
  • Aston Martin DBX707: Extremely fast but lacks the futuristic hybrid technology and brand craze that Lamborghini has in India.
  • Verdict: The Urus SE is the most balanced. It offers the best tech, the best service network in India, and the highest status.

11. Maintenance and Charging in India

Owning a Lamborghini in India is easier now than it was 10 years ago. Lamborghini has service centers in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore. For the Urus SE, you will need a 7kW home charger, which Lamborghini will install at your home or office. It takes about 3-4 hours to charge the battery fully.

Service Cost: Expect to pay around ₹2.5 Lakh to ₹4 Lakh per year for regular servicing and oil changes.
