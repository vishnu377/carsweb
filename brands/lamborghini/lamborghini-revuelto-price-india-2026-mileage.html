1. The 2026 Price Reality: Why ₹10 Crore?

The Lamborghini Revuelto is the successor to the legendary Aventador. When it was launched, it was priced around ₹8.89 Crore (Ex-showroom). However, in April 2026, with the new luxury cess and custom duties on high-capacity engines, the on-road price in cities like Mumbai and Bangalore has officially crossed the ₹10 Crore mark.

But why is it so expensive? It’s not just a car; it’s a HPEV (High Performance Electric Vehicle). It features a massive 6.5-litre V12 engine paired with three electric motors. This combination produces a mind-boggling 1015 CV of power. You are not just paying for the car; you are paying for the most advanced hybrid technology ever created by Lamborghini.

5. Performance: Is it the Fastest Car in the World?

While the Revuelto is lightning fast (0-100 km/h in 2.5 seconds), it is technically not the "Fastest Car in the World" in 2026. That title still belongs to monsters like the Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut or the Bugatti Bolide. However, for Indian roads, the Lamborghini Revuelto is arguably the most "usable" supercar because of its lift-suspension system that helps it clear those giant Indian speed breakers.

6. The Future: Lamborghini Lanzador EV

Lamborghini has confirmed that the roar of the V12 is slowly coming to an end. The Lamborghini Lanzador, their first all-electric Ultra-GT, is already seeing a 170% increase in search interest. It promises over 1300 HP. If you are a fan of the "Sound," 2026 might be your last chance to buy a V12 Lamborghini before everything goes silent.

7. Ownership and Maintenance: The Reality

Buying a Lamborghini is easy for billionaires, but maintaining it is the real challenge. A single service for a Revuelto in 2026 can cost between ₹3 Lakh to ₹5 Lakh. If you accidentally hit a pothole and damage a rim, replacing it can cost you the price of a Maruti Swift (approx ₹8 Lakh)!
