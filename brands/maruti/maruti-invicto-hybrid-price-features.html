1. The Power of Strong Hybrid

The Invicto is powered by a **2.0L 4-Cylinder VVTi Engine** coupled with an electric motor. Unlike the Ciaz's mild-hybrid, this is a **Strong Hybrid** system. It can run on pure electric mode (EV mode) for short distances, making it pin-drop silent in city traffic.

With a combined power output of 184 bhp, it offers effortless acceleration. It comes with an e-CVT transmission, ensuring a jerk-free driving experience.

2. Business Class Interior & Comfort

Step inside, and the 'Champagne Gold' accents will catch your eye. The Invicto is designed for those who prefer being chauffeur-driven. It comes in 7-seater (Captain Seats) and 8-seater configurations.

  • Panoramic Sunroof: A massive dual-pane sunroof for an airy feel.
  • Ventilated Front Seats: Stay cool even in the hottest Indian summers.
  • Dual Zone AC: Separate climate control for front and rear passengers.
  • Power Tailgate: Open the massive boot with just a click of a button.

4. Safety: Built to Protect

The Invicto is built on the TNGA-K platform, which is known for its high structural integrity. It comes loaded with safety tech:

  • 6 Airbags: Standard across all variants.
  • 360-Degree Camera: Essential for parking a car of this size.
  • TPMS: Tyre Pressure Monitoring System.
  • Disc Brakes: On all four wheels for superior stopping power.
  • VSC & Hill Hold: Vehicle Stability Control for highway safety.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Is Invicto better than Toyota Hycross?

Ans. Both are essentially the same car. Hycross offers ADAS and an Ottoman seat, while Invicto is slightly more affordable and has a different grill design.

Q2. Does it need external charging?

Ans. No, it is a self-charging hybrid. The battery charges automatically through the engine and regenerative braking.

Q3. What is the real-world mileage?

Ans. In city traffic, you can easily get 18-20 kmpl, which is amazing for a car of this weight.
