1. Technical Evolution: The Heart of the Eeco

The 2025 Maruti Eeco is powered by the highly reliable **1.2L Advanced K-Series Dual Jet, Dual VVT engine**. This is a major upgrade from the old G12 engine. The new engine is not only smoother but also provides better thermal efficiency.

For a vehicle that often carries heavy loads (7 people or business cargo), the 80.7 PS of power is surprisingly adequate. The low-end torque is tuned for city driving, meaning you won't have to shift gears constantly in traffic.

2. Fuel Efficiency: Why It's the 'Savings King'

When you buy an Eeco, you care about two things: Space and Mileage. Maruti knows this perfectly. The 2025 model offers some of the best fuel economy figures in the utility segment:

  • Petrol Variants: 19.71 kmpl (ARAI Certified)
  • CNG Variants: 26.78 km/kg (ARAI Certified)

In our **VishTech Real-World Test**, the Eeco CNG delivered a solid 24 km/kg on highways with a full load, which is incredible for a car of this size.

3. 5-Seater vs 7-Seater: Confusion Solved!

One of the most common questions we get at VishTech is: "Should I buy the 5-seater or the 7-seater?" Here is the breakdown:

The 5-Seater AC: This is best for small families who need a massive boot (luggage) space. It’s perfect if you travel with 4-5 people and have a lot of bags. It also comes with an Air Conditioner.

The 7-Seater Standard: This is for large joint families. It has a bench seat in the middle and a small bench at the back. Note: The 7-seater does NOT come with factory-fitted AC to keep the price low and engine load manageable.

4. Safety: Is the Eeco Safe for Your Family?

Historically, vans weren't known for safety. However, to meet the latest Indian Safety Norms (AIS), Maruti has updated the Eeco with 11+ safety features:

  • Dual Airbags: Driver and co-driver side airbags are now standard.
  • ABS with EBD: Prevents skidding during emergency braking.
  • Illuminated Hazard Switch: For better visibility in the dark.
  • Reverse Parking Sensors: A must-have for a long van like this.
  • Child Lock: On the sliding doors for peace of mind.

7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1. Can we fit a music system in the Eeco?

Ans. Yes, Maruti provides a 1-DIN space for music systems. You can easily install an aftermarket touchscreen or a basic Sony/Pioneer system.

Q2. Is Eeco good for long highway trips?

Ans. It is okay for 200-300km trips, but because it lacks power steering and has thin tires, it can feel a bit unstable at speeds above 100kmph. 80kmph is the sweet spot.

Q3. Does Eeco have a CNG Tank indicator?

Ans. Yes, the new digital instrument cluster shows the CNG level and the fuel mode (Petrol/CNG) clearly.
