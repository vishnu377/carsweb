2. The Game Changer: 1.2L Turbo CNG Engine

Most CNG cars in India feel slow. This is because they use "Naturally Aspirated" engines. Tata has changed the game by offering a Turbo-Charged CNG Engine in the Curvv. This is a massive update for 2026.

When you press the accelerator, the turbo kicks in. You don't feel that "power loss" that people usually complain about in CNG cars. Whether you are climbing a mountain or overtaking a truck on the highway, the Curvv CNG feels like a powerful petrol car.

4. Boot Space: Where Did the Tanks Go?

In 2026, Tata's Twin Cylinder Technology has become the gold standard. In the Curvv CNG, you won't find a huge cylinder eating up your luggage space. Instead, there are two smaller tanks placed deep under the luggage floor.

You get a massive **320 liters of usable boot space**. This is enough for two large suitcases and a couple of soft bags. For a family going on a weekend trip to Manali or Lonavala, this is a lifesaver. You no longer have to choose between "Cheaper Fuel" and "Family Luggage."

7. Running Costs: The Financial Logic

Let's do some simple math for 2026 fuel prices. If you drive 2,000 km every month:

Petrol SUV: 2,000 km / 14 kmpl = 142 Liters. 142 x ₹105 = ₹14,910 per month.

Curvv CNG: 2,000 km / 24 km/kg = 83 kg. 83 x ₹88 = ₹7,304 per month.

Your Monthly Saving: ₹7,606!

Over a period of 3 years, you will save almost ₹2.7 Lakhs. That is the price of a small car! Essentially, the Curvv pays for its own premium price tag through fuel savings.

8. Driving Experience: Manual or Automatic?

In 2026, Tata has introduced the DCA (Dual Clutch Automatic) with CNG. This is a world-first. If you live in a city with heavy traffic, the Automatic version is a blessing. The gear shifts are fast and smooth. You won't even realize the car is running on CNG.

The manual version is also great for those who love control. The 6-speed gearbox is light, and the clutch is soft, making it easy for daily commuting.
