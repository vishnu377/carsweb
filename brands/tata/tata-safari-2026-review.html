1. Exterior Design: The Road Presence

In 2026, the Safari has received a major facelift. The front looks very futuristic with a connected LED light bar that runs across the bonnet. It performs a beautiful animation when you unlock the car. The grille now has body-colored inserts which make it look more premium than the older models.

The New 20-Inch Wheels

One of the biggest changes is the wheel size. The top-end variants now come with 20-inch alloy wheels. These wheels fill the arches perfectly and give the Safari a stance that can challenge the Toyota Fortuner. The ground clearance remains a solid 205mm, which is more than enough for Indian potholes and flooded roads.

2. Real Mileage: The "Kitna Deti Hai" Test

This is what every Indian buyer wants to know. Tata claims a high number, but we tested it in real-world conditions. We used the "Tank-to-Tank" method for 100% accuracy.

Driving Condition 2026 Safari Diesel (AT) 2026 Safari Petrol (MT)
City Traffic (Bumper-to-Bumper)11.2 kmpl9.5 kmpl
Highway (Cruising at 90kmph)17.4 kmpl14.8 kmpl
Mountain/Hills (Steep Climb)8.9 kmpl7.2 kmpl
Overall Average13.5 kmpl11.4 kmpl

For a car that weighs nearly 2 tons and carries 7 people, these numbers are very respectable. If you drive with a light foot in "Eco Mode," you can easily get 18 kmpl on the highway.

3. The 7-Seater Test: Is the 3rd Row Usable?

Many SUVs claim to be 7-seaters, but the last row is usually only for kids. We put two adults (5'8" tall) in the 3rd row for a 2-hour journey.

The result? They were surprisingly comfortable! Tata has added a "Boss Mode" button that slides the middle-row seat forward, creating more legroom in the back. The 3rd row also gets its own AC vents with fan control, separate USB charging ports, and even cup holders. If you have a large family, the Safari is a much better choice than the Mahindra XUV700 in terms of last-row space.

4. Engine and Performance: The AWD Power

Under the hood, the 2026 Safari uses the updated 2.0L Kryotec Diesel engine producing 170 BHP and 350 Nm of torque. But the big news for 2026 is the Electronic AWD system.

How does it drive?

The engine is now much quieter than before. The 6-speed Torque Converter automatic gearbox is smooth and doesn't feel jerky in traffic. When we took the AWD version on a muddy track, the car automatically shifted power to the wheels with the most grip. It is not a hardcore off-roader like the Thar, but it can easily handle snow, mud, and sand during your vacations.

5. Luxury and Technology: A Step Ahead

Inside the cabin, you are greeted by a 14.1-inch HARMAN touchscreen. It is fast, clear, and supports wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay. The 10-speaker JBL sound system is the best in the segment—it feels like a concert hall inside the car.

  • Ventilated Seats: Available in both 1st and 2nd row (Captain seats).
  • Panoramic Sunroof: The largest in the class with "Voice Control."
  • ADAS Level 2.5: Includes Adaptive Cruise, Blind Spot Monitoring, and Lane Keep Assist.

Why You Should Buy It:

  • Incredible road presence—people will give you way.
  • Best-in-class 3rd row comfort for adults.
  • Solid 5-star Bharat NCAP safety rating.
  • The new AWD system adds great confidence on bad roads.

Things to Improve:

  • The 20-inch wheels make the ride a bit stiff on sharp bumps.
  • The steering is still a bit heavy at very low speeds.
  • No Petrol engine option for those who want low vibrations (though rumored).
