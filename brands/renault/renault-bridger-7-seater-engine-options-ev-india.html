I clearly remember when Renault first launched the Duster. It was a game-changer for India. But today, people want more space, more features, and more engine choices.

Yesterday, when the news about the **Renault Bridger** (internationally known as the Bigster) broke out, I was excited. This isn't just a car; it’s a massive 7-seater that looks like it could survive an apocalypse. If you were planning to buy an XUV700 or a Safari, you must read this first!

The **Renault Bridger** is the bigger brother of the upcoming new Duster. It is designed for big families who love to travel together. The most amazing part? Renault is not just giving one engine; they are giving three different types of engines and even a full Electric version! Let's dive into the details.

1. Three Powerful Engine Options

Renault knows that every driver is different. That is why they are offering three choices for the Bridger:

  • The 1.2L Turbo Petrol (Mild Hybrid): This is for people who want a balance of power and efficiency. It uses a 48V hybrid system to help with fuel economy in city traffic. It produces about 130 hp.
  • The 1.6L Strong Hybrid: This is the most advanced engine. It has two electric motors and can run on pure electricity for up to 80% of city driving. It offers excellent mileage, similar to a diesel car.
  • The 1.2L Turbo with 4x4: For off-road lovers, Renault is offering an All-Wheel Drive (AWD) version. It is perfect for driving through mud, snow, or mountains.

2. Bridger EV: The Electric Future

Renault has also confirmed that a **Bridger EV** is coming. This will be a great option for people who want to save money on petrol.

The EV version is expected to have a 60 kWh battery. You could get a driving range of about 450 km on a single charge. It will have a very modern interior and will compete with the Tata Safari EV and Mahindra XEV 9e.

3. Design: Bold and Rugged

The Bridger looks very tough. It is 4.6 meters long, which makes it a proper 7-seater SUV. The front has the new 'Y' shaped LED lights that look very futuristic.

Renault has used a special material called **Starkle®** for the body cladding. It is made from recycled plastic and is very hard to scratch. This makes the car perfect for rough Indian roads where bikes and scooters often rub against cars.

4. Interior and Comfort

The interior of the Bridger is very simple but very high-tech. The dashboard is tilted towards the driver for a better view.

  • Panoramic Sunroof: It makes the big car feel even bigger from the inside.
  • YouClip System: This is a cool feature! You can easily clip your phone, tablet, or a bag hook anywhere on the dashboard or seats.
  • Huge Boot Space: Even with 7 seats, there is enough room for your luggage. If you fold the last row, it becomes a mini-truck!

5. Safety and Features

Safety is a big priority for Renault. The Bridger will come with 6 Airbags as standard. It will also have **ADAS Level 2**, which means the car can automatically apply brakes if it detects a crash or keep itself in the lane on the highway.

Pros: Why should you buy it?

  • Fuel Efficiency: The Strong Hybrid version will save you a lot of money.
