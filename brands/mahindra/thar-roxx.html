Mahindra Thar Roxx 2026: The Ultimate Swag Meets Family Comfort

By: Auto News Editorial Updated: Jan 2, 2026
"Bhai, Jaipur to Delhi ki trip Thar Roxx mein hi karenge!" — Just the other day, my friend said this to me. When I asked him why he’s so crazy about this car, he replied, "It's for people who want 'Tora' (Swag) and safety for their family at the same time." It’s amazing how Mahindra has turned a rugged off-roader into a practical 5-door family beast.

The Mahindra Thar Roxx is not just a car; it's a statement. With its new 5-door avatar, it has solved the biggest problem of the old Thar—space. Now, your family doesn't have to struggle to get into the back seat, making it the perfect companion for long road trips like our Jaipur-Delhi run.

'Khatarnak' Looks: Ruggedness Re-defined

From the outside, the Roxx looks absolutely 'Khatarnak' (dangerous). Mahindra has given it a new 6-slot grille and C-shaped LED DRLs that give it a sharp, modern identity. But the real show-stoppers are the 19-inch diamond-cut alloy wheels. They give the car a heavy, muscular stance that commands respect the moment you stop at a red light.

The Interior: Luxury That Impressed My Girlfriend!

If you think the Thar is still just a "metal box" inside, you're wrong. When my girlfriend saw the interior, she was genuinely surprised. The premium white-themed cabin feels like a luxury lounge.

The massive 10.25-inch touchscreen handles all the music and navigation, while the Panoramic Sunroof makes the cabin feel huge. And let's not forget the 9-speaker Harman Kardon system—it's like having a DJ on wheels. For the burning heat of North India, the ventilated seats were a complete lifesaver!

Thar Roxx Quick Specs:
  • Engine: 2.0L Petrol / 2.2L Diesel options.
  • Drive: RWD for the city, 4WD (4x4) for the mountains.
  • Safety: 6 Airbags (Standard) and Level 2 ADAS.
  • Comfort: Ventilated Seats & Panoramic Sunroof.

Safety: More Than Just Muscle

Safety was our biggest concern for the highway. The Thar Roxx comes with 6 Airbags as standard across all variants. It also features Level 2 ADAS, which helps the car stay in its lane and even apply emergency brakes if needed. It’s a huge relief when you’re cruising at 100 kmph on the Delhi-Jaipur Expressway.

The Verdict: Worth the Price?

Starting at ₹12.99 Lakh and going up to ₹22.49 Lakh, the Thar Roxx is giving tough competition to SUVs like the Creta and Seltos. If you want a car that has the heart of an off-roader but the soul of a luxury car, this is it. As my friend said, "No matter what, this car wins the heart."
