Maruti Dzire Hybrid vs Honda Amaze 2026: 35kmpl Mileage or ADAS Safety?

By: VishTechCars Editorial Team Updated: Jan 18, 2026
"We all work hard for our money. When you buy a car in 2026, you're not just buying metal; you're buying security for your children and savings for your future."

With petrol prices touching new highs on January 18, 2026, the **Maruti Dzire Hybrid** and **New Honda Amaze** are the two biggest rivals. One wants to save every drop of fuel, while the other wants to keep you safe with advanced technology. Let's find out which sedan truly deserves your hard-earned money.

The Indian car market has changed. Today, buyers don't just ask "How much does it cost?" They ask "How much will it save me?" and "How safe is my family inside?" If you have a budget of ₹8 Lakh to ₹12 Lakh, the choice between the 2026 Dzire Hybrid and the New Amaze is the toughest decision you will make this year.

1. The Mileage War: Can Anyone Beat 35 kmpl?

Let's talk about the biggest reason people buy Maruti: **Mileage.** The 2026 Maruti Dzire is now a **Strong Hybrid.** This means it has a large battery and an electric motor that helps the engine.

In heavy city traffic, the Dzire Hybrid can give you a mind-blowing **32 to 35 kmpl.** Imagine driving from your home to the office for a whole week and barely seeing the fuel needle move! It starts silently on electric power, making you feel like you're driving a luxury EV.

The **Honda Amaze 2026**, however, sticks to its 1.2L i-VTEC petrol engine. It is smooth and reliable, but it cannot match the Hybrid's numbers. You will get around **18-19 kmpl.** If your main goal is to save ₹50,000 every year on petrol, the Dzire is the king of the road.

2. Safety: The Peace of Mind You Need

Money is important, but life is priceless. This is where the **New Honda Amaze 2026** shines. For the first time, Honda has brought **ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems)** to the Amaze.

What does this mean for you? If a car suddenly stops in front of you, the Amaze can apply the brakes automatically. If you accidentally drift out of your lane, it can steer you back. It's like having a guardian angel watching over your family. Honda Sensing technology makes the Amaze one of the safest sedans ever built in India.

The Maruti Dzire Hybrid has also improved. It now comes with 6 Airbags and a much stronger chassis. But it still lacks that "heavy" build quality feel that Honda provides. If you travel on highways with your kids and parents, the Amaze gives you that extra layer of confidence.

3. Interiors and Comfort: Luxury at Home

Stepping inside the **Honda Amaze 2026** feels like stepping into a premium hotel. The dashboard is clean, the materials feel soft, and the seats are wide. It's designed for people who want to feel relaxed after a long day at work. The Amaze also offers better legroom for tall passengers in the back.

The **Maruti Dzire Hybrid** interior is more futuristic. It has a **Head-Up Display (HUD)** that shows your speed on the glass, so you never have to look down. It also features a 360-degree camera which makes parking in tight spots very easy. While it's tech-loaded, the cabin doesn't feel as "premium" or "solid" as the Amaze.

4. Running Costs: The 5-Year Calculation

Let's do some simple math. If you drive 1,000 km every month:

  • **Maruti Dzire Hybrid:** You will spend roughly ₹3,000 on petrol.
  • **Honda Amaze Petrol:** You will spend roughly ₹5,500 on petrol.
In 5 years, the Dzire Hybrid can save you over **₹1.5 Lakh!** This is money you could spend on your child's education or a family vacation. However, the Amaze is slightly cheaper to buy upfront, so you save money at the time of purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is the Dzire Hybrid expensive to maintain?
No, Maruti has optimized the battery life, and they offer an 8-year warranty on the hybrid system. Service costs remain low.
2. Can the Honda Amaze drive itself with ADAS?
It is not a self-driving car, but it helps with lane keeping and emergency braking, making highway driving much safer and less tiring.
3. Which car has a better automatic transmission?
Honda's CVT is much smoother than Maruti's system. If you want a jerk-free driving experience, Amaze is better.

Final Verdict: Which one is for you?

After driving both cars extensively in January 2026, our verdict is simple:

  • Choose the Maruti Dzire Hybrid if: You are a smart saver. You drive a lot in the city and want the highest mileage in India. You want a car that has a great resale value.
  • Choose the Honda Amaze 2026 if: You are a safety-conscious parent. You want a car that looks and feels like a luxury sedan. You want the advanced protection of ADAS for highway trips.
